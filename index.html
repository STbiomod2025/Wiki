Arbitrary planar design using avidin-biotin binding

We propose a new tool for bending DNA origami structures using the avidin–biotin interaction — stable and tunable.

Background

DNA origami is a technique that constructs nanoscale structures by combining circular DNA with numerous short single-stranded DNA (ssDNA) molecules. Current strategies for bending DNA origami include methods that induce curvature in response to ionic concentration and those that employ ssDNA.

  1. P. W. K. Rothemund, Nature, 440, 297–302 (2006)
  2. Suzuki, Y. et al., Angew. Chem. Int. Ed., 59, 6230-6234 (2020)
  3. J. Funke, H. Dietz, Nature Nanotech, 11, 47–52 (2016)

Problem
Existing DNA origami bending methods depend on ionic concentration or specific ssDNA sequences, making them unstable and complex.

Learn more in Simulation

Idea

We propose a new tool for bending DNA origami structures using the avidin–biotin interaction. Biotin-modified thymine bases linked by carbon spacers bend when avidin binds causing precise curvature control.

Learn more in Experiment

Vision

When multiple thymine bases in planar DNA origami are modified by biotin, the addition of avidin induces the formation of a three-dimensional curved surface composed of multiple combined bends.
