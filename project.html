Our Research Project

Introduction

DNA origami enables the creation of nanoscale structures by folding DNA into designed shapes. This technology has opened new possibilities in nanodevices, molecular computing, and synthetic biology.

However, current bending techniques using ionic conditions or single-stranded DNA (ssDNA) linkers often suffer from low stability and complex design requirements.Moreover, environmental factors such as pH and temperature can easily disrupt the structures.

To overcome these limitations, we propose a novel method for bending DNA origami using the highly stable and specific avidin–biotin interaction. This approach allows precise and robust curvature control through simple biotin modification, providing a new tool for constructing durable DNA origami architectures independent of environmental conditions such as ion concentration.
